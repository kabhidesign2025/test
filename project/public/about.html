About The ART Foundation

India’s leading platform that transforms creativity into meaningful impact since 2011

The ART Foundation was established in 2011 by Mr. Pandu Ranga Guptha with the vision to empower children through artistic expression. With over a decade of dedication, it has become India’s largest creative platform promoting peace, sustainability, and education through art.

We connect students, educators, and schools from all across the country through national competitions, community events, webinars, and art education programs aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

The foundation continues to ignite imagination, raise awareness, and celebrate talent — nurturing over 5000 schools and empowering countless students to create change through creativity.

Mr. Pandu Ranga Guptha

Founder & Chairman, The ART Foundation

SDG Impact Award

Awarded by the United Nations for promoting sustainability through education and art

Mr. Pandu Ranga Guptha

Honored with the prestigious SDG Impact Award by the United Nations, Mr. Guptha has been recognized globally for his outstanding contribution in spreading awareness about the Sustainable Development Goals among school children through art and education.

His visionary leadership at The ART Foundation has created platforms for over 5000 schools to embrace creativity as a tool for sustainability and peace-building. This international recognition marks a proud moment in India’s educational and creative journey.

Our Journey of Impact

A timeline of creativity, community, and recognition since 2011

2011

The ART Foundation was founded to promote peace and creativity through art.

2012

Hosted the 1st National Level Drawing Competition with 500+ schools nationwide.

2013

Expanded recognition across multiple states and regions.

2014

Introduced SDG-themed art competitions across schools.

2015

Launched mentorship programs in collaboration with leading institutions.

2016

Created the TAF Art Community Platform for students and teachers.

2017

Engaged 2000+ schools through competitions and workshops.

2018

Launched creative SDG campaigns for awareness through art.

2019

Adopted online competitions and nationwide webinar series.

2020

Continued digital events and workshops during the pandemic.

2021

Promoted art curriculum to 4000+ schools.

2022

Crossed 5000+ registered schools in our art programs.

2023

Launched structured art classes and online community drives.

2024

Archived national winner galleries and introduced student showcases.

2025

Received the UN SDG Impact Award for sustainability education through art.
